Key Features

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence by machines. In this project, AI plays a pivotal role in detecting brain tumors by enabling the system to analyze medical MRI images and classify them as either "Tumor Detected" or "No Tumor Detected". The AI model is trained using a dataset of labeled MRI scans, enabling it to learn patterns and make accurate predictions without human intervention. This not only reduces diagnostic time but also increases reliability in medical image analysis.

Read more
Deep Learning (DL)

Deep Learning is a powerful subset of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that mimics the functioning of the human brain processing large amounts of data. It uses neural networks with multiple layers (deep neural networks) to automatically learn features from raw data like images, videos, or text without the need for manual feature extraction. Deep learning empowers your model to identify even complex tumor patterns and distinguish between normal and abnormal scans, making the diagnosis faster, more reliable, and intelligent — especially important in critical healthcare applications.

Read more
Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)

CNN (Convolutional Neural Network) is a deep learning algorithm highly effective in image classification and pattern recognition tasks. In this project, CNN is used to build and train the brain tumor detection model. The network consists of multiple layers such as convolutional layers, activation layers, pooling layers, and fully connected layers, which work together to automatically extract features from MRI images. The CNN model learns to differentiate between tumor and non-tumor images through a process called backpropagation during training, thereby achieving high accuracy in tumor detection.
